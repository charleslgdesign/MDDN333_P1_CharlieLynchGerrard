The purpose of this visualization was to gain more control over my limited time as a student. A comparitive display of the time in ellipses really helped me examine the differences between each application. Each circle's size (radius) is a total of all the 20 days multiplied by 15.

Personally, I think this reflects on the regularity of my schedule. I find that having a consistant schedule helps me complete tasks, especially those that are drawn out over multiple days/weeks.

The scavolding used was very minimalist. This was intentional as the data isn't displayed in a typical graph format. I decided to animate my visualization to show the true scale of the ellipses. This is because the data could be interpreted differently considering the area of the circles overlap.

Possible future applications of this visualization could be more in-depth comparisons between specific days, rather than only the total over the 20-day period.
